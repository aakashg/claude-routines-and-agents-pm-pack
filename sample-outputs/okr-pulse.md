## What this produces in the Monday meeting

- **Activation:** team confirms they're closing out First-run checklist this week. Keeps.
- **NPS:** conversation about why Support response time is the only active lever. Someone raises the idea of adding a self-serve knowledge base initiative. Action item: Sarah to scope by Friday.
- **Enterprise integrations:** awkward silence. Nothing active on a KR we committed to. Decision: either kill the KR or assign by EOD. Forces the call.
- **Orphaned work:** three items with real effort going in but no KR connection. Conversation: should Dashboard mobile be a KR addition? Or is it correctly a non-goal this quarter?

## Why this format works

- **No opinions, just data.** The agent doesn't say "this is bad." It says "no active work." The team draws the conclusion.
- **The orphaned work section is the most valuable.** That's where surprises live.
- **"Full initiative breakdown available on request"** keeps the main report scannable. If someone wants detail, they ask.

## Tuning note

The first few weeks will surface orphaned work that makes people defensive. ("It's connected to KR 2 informally.") Either formalize the connection in the PM tool or accept that it's correctly non-goal. Don't let the agent be gaslit into fuzzy mappings.
